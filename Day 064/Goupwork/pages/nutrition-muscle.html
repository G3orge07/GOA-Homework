Stretching & Mobility Routine

Recovery isn’t just about nutrition and sleep—proper stretching and mobility work can prevent injuries and improve performance. After a workout, dynamic stretches help reduce muscle tightness, while static stretches improve flexibility. Try a simple routine:

  1. Child’s Pose (30 seconds) – Stretches lower back and shoulders.
  2. Hip Flexor Stretch (30 seconds per side) – Loosens tight hips from sitting or squatting.
  3. Seated Hamstring Stretch (30 seconds per leg) – Improves flexibility in the legs.
  4. Chest Opener Stretch (30 seconds) – Helps release tight chest muscles.
  5. Foam Rolling (2-3 minutes) – A great way to massage and release tight spots in muscles.

Spending just 5-10 minutes after your workout on mobility can make a huge difference in reducing soreness and improving flexibility.

Sleep & Rest

Muscle recovery doesn’t happen in the gym—it happens when you rest. Sleep is when your body repairs muscle tissue, balances hormones, and reduces inflammation. Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night to maximize recovery. Establishing a consistent sleep schedule, avoiding screens before bed, and practicing relaxation techniques can improve sleep quality, helping your muscles recover faster and your workouts feel easier.

Post-Workout Meal

After an intense workout, your muscles need protein and carbs to repair and grow. A perfect recovery meal is a protein-packed smoothie with banana, Greek yogurt, peanut butter, and honey. The Greek yogurt provides high-quality protein, bananas help replenish glycogen, and peanut butter adds healthy fats for sustained energy. It’s quick, easy, and ideal for post-workout recovery.
